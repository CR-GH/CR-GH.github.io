Computing Concepts

The third lesson includes more information on how computers work, this time teaching the basics of how computers break down and solve complex problems. This lesson introduces four key concepts, those being Decomposition (where one complex problem is divided into multiple simpler steps), Pattern Recognition (where similar aspects of different problems are identified), Algorithms (where a sequence of steps to solve a particular problem is created), and Abstraction (where a solution is generalized so that it can be applied to multiple problems).

For my submission, I asked about how descriptive, predictive, and prescriptive analyses were typically performed in a business environment, then received an example from a peer of how these processes can occur in repair facilities.

IST and Society

In Lesson 12, the various societal issues IST can bring about are introduced. Concepts like the "digital divide," where financial and educational differences in a population can potentially limit access to some technology and create a divide between those who do have this access and those who are unable to do so, are explained. Additionally, the issue of cybercrime and how various aspects of the Internet such as anonymity can further complicate this issue is discussed in this lesson.


Project Showcase - Cybercrime

For this project, we were tasked with exploring some of the legal issues surrounding cybercrime. For my project, I aimed to answer the question of whether or not punishments for cybercrimes should be more severe for repeat offenders, to which I decided that the answer to this should depend on what kind of crime was committed. As cybercrime can range from activities such as an individual viewing illegal content on dark web sites to a large group carrying out a complex hack of a major corporation, more severe punishments for repeat offenders are more likely to deter those committing crimes that require less knowledge of computers as they do not have the experience to avoid being caught; however, severe punishments for repeat offenders may not be as effective for experienced hackers, who may instead respond by bolstering their knowledge to avoid detection.

Moving on from the societal dilemmas of IST, this lesson focuses on more business-related issues. Project Showcase - Crowdsourcing

For this discussion project, our goal was to explore crowdsourcing in more detail. For my project, I defined crowdsourcing and analyzed one particular field that has recently benefited significantly from crowdsourcing, that being the indie games industry. With the increasing popularity of indie games--video games made by smaller development teams--I found that crowdsourcing has become an increasingly attractive way for developers to refine their games, as a willing crowd can be very effective in quickly locating bugs as well as sharing feedback on what works and what doesn't in a game.
